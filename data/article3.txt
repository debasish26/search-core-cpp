Title : Climate Change

Climate change refers to long term changes in global temperatures and weather patterns. While natural processes have influenced Earth's climate throughout history, scientific evidence shows that recent warming trends are largely driven by human activities.

The primary cause of modern climate change is the increased concentration of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere. These gases, such as carbon dioxide and methane, trap heat and prevent it from escaping into space. Burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes contribute significantly to greenhouse gas emissions.

The consequences of climate change include rising sea levels, more frequent extreme weather events, and shifts in ecosystems. Glaciers and polar ice caps are melting, and many species are being forced to adapt to changing environmental conditions.

Scientists and policymakers are working together to address climate change through mitigation and adaptation strategies. Mitigation focuses on reducing greenhouse gas emissions by transitioning to renewable energy sources such as solar and wind power. Adaptation involves preparing communities to cope with the impacts that are already occurring.

Public awareness and international cooperation are essential for tackling climate change. Global agreements encourage countries to set targets for reducing emissions and investing in sustainable technologies.
